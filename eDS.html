Etomon Design System

Build a design system from scratch

Type

Self-initiated project at work


Duration

04/2019 - Present

My Role

Build & Maintain style guide


Tools

Figma

Background

Etomon is an online platform for students to learn with teachers 1-on-1 in real time. During my internship at Etomon, I found that there lack a design system for the team to produce consistent designs efficiently. So I took the initiative to build and maintain the style guide for Etomon's product.

Understand the branding

Etomon aims to create a people-centered community where students are cared about by their instructors as they learn and grow together. The main users are teenagers over 15 years old and adults aged up to 35. An energetic, clean, and friendly visual style would perfectly fit the product.

Typography

A New Font

I started with setting up the font and its hierarchies because it was the main issue at that time. Every designer was using their own font sizes and weights and the old fonts didn't go well with Etomon's branding.

I set up 6 criteria for the potential new fonts and did a thorough search on Google Fonts and Fonts Squirrel. As a result, I found 2 fonts that meet the standards: Noto Sans and Nunito. I then coded 2*2 webpages to for the team to choose the best version.

The Scale

Version 1 (Noto Sans for all texts) was the winner. I then moved on to created type hierarchies and had it tested with several main pages. I chose 16px as the base size, and 1.125 as the ratio after trying different commonly used ratios. Then I tweaked the scale to better fit Etomon's product.

Colors

A New Color

Previously, Etomon's theme color was a deep turquoise which looks unique at the first sight, however, most people find it difficult to relate the color to the education theme and it doesn't provide enough contrast on a white background. So after discussing with team, I set out to search for a new theme color.

Again, we were looking for a color that makes people feel energetic, clean, and friendly. Plus, it is expected to be associated with the learning theme as perceived by most people. With these in mind, I finally picked 4 potential colors and tried them on our product.

This time I used surveys to help us make the decision. I sent questionnaires containing these 4 different color themes and our product introduction to our potential users and collected 237 feedbacks. As a result, we chose the green as our new theme color considering it is most liked and people's impression of it matched with our visual style keywords.

Color Palette

Our primary palette is comprised of our logo green, a dark green, and white. The dark green was added so that we could use green on texts while conforming to WCAG 2.0. Orange is used for warnings and alerts and red is for notification.

Spacing

A spacing system is important because it creates a rhythm for the page, helps people understand information relationships, and it contributes to the product's visual identity. I created the vertical spacing scale while experimenting with different spacings on many pages, and finally decided on the 10-level spacing scale. For horizontal spacings, a 12-column grid is applied to laptop versions while the margin for each page may vary depending on the page's information desity.
